Plates Push. Rocks Store Stress. Faults Break. Earthquakes Begin.

A self-learning experience for describing how tectonic plate movement provides the stress that shifts and breaks rocks beneath Earth’s surface, forming faults and causing earthquakes.

One Chain of Cause and Effect

Earth’s lithosphere is broken into moving plates. Their movement is slow, but it can squeeze, pull, or shear rocks. When stress becomes greater than the rock’s strength, the rock breaks or slips along a fault. The sudden release of stored energy sends seismic waves outward as an earthquake.

1. Plate Motion

Stress Starts

Convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries create different kinds of stress underground.

2. Rock Response

Rocks Bend, Then Break

Rocks can deform slowly, but stored elastic energy rises when movement is blocked.

3. Earthquake

Sudden Slip

When the fault slips, energy travels as seismic waves from the focus toward surrounding areas.

A

Compressional stress squeezes rock and commonly forms reverse faults.

B

Tensional stress pulls rock apart and commonly forms normal faults.

C

Shear stress moves rock sideways and commonly forms strike-slip faults.
